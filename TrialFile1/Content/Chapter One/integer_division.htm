integer division

Strict typing plays a role in integer division as well. When integers are divided, the remainder is discarded. The results may not be what one would expect. 4/3 is equal to 1. For exact results, use floating point types.

Java also provides an operation called modulus, which calculates the remainder of integer division. For example, 4 % 2 is equal to 0, as there is no remainder. 3 % 2 is equal to 1, the value of the remainder. Modulus is useful in a variety of contexts, e.g., dividing up objects among a group or for production cycles, when the remaining quantity of goods or materials is sought.
